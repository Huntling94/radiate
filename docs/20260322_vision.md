# Radiate — Product Vision

> An idle evolution ecosystem builder with genuine emergent simulation and a living social metagame.

**Version:** 1.0 · **Date:** 22 March 2026

---

## Vision statement

A browser-based idle game where you shape a world's environment and watch evolution unfold over geological timescales. The simulation runs while you're away. When you return, you discover what happened: new species branched, old ones went extinct, arms races shifted, ecosystems restructured. You make strategic decisions in short active bursts — reshape terrain, trigger events, manage crises — then let nature take its course again. Your world's species can migrate to other players' worlds, creating a living social ecosystem where the most robust organisms spread across the network and every world tells a unique evolutionary story.

**One-line pitch:** Factorio meets a nature documentary, compressed into 10 minutes a day, where the factory is an ecosystem and the machines are evolving creatures.

**Core insight:** Evolution is already an idle game. It's a passive process with punctuated moments of crisis and opportunity. No one has designed a game that embraces this structure.

---

## The three layers

### Solo layer
You shape a world, seed life, and watch evolution unfold. The satisfaction is designing a system and watching it work — the same appeal as tending a garden, building a Factorio base, or maintaining an aquarium. The idle format means the world keeps going while you live your life.

### Social layer
Your world's species can leave and enter other worlds. You browse a gallery of creatures other players evolved, introduce them to your ecosystem, and track outcomes. Species develop reputations across the player base. The metagame is ecological, not competitive.

### Discovery layer
The phylogenetic tree, the bestiary, the naturalist field notes, the convergent evolution tracker, the robustness leaderboard — these create a shared body of knowledge about what works in this game's evolutionary physics. The community collectively maps the fitness landscape.

## Game loop

### Dual-timescale design

| Layer | Timescale | Player action | Emotional payoff |
|-------|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| Passive simulation | Runs continuously (hours/days) | None — world evolves autonomously | Anticipation, curiosity, surprise on return |
| Active observation | 5–15 min check-ins | Review changes, study species, tag lineages | Discovery, understanding, attachment |
| Strategic intervention | Periodic decisions | Reshape terrain, trigger events, introduce species | Agency, consequence, experimentation |
| Long-term progression | Weeks to months | Complete ecological challenges, unlock tools, curate world museum | Mastery, collection, narrative arc |

### Progression system

**Short-term (minutes to hours):** Population shifts, new mutations, small adaptations. "My herbivore just evolved camouflage."

**Medium-term (days to weeks):** Major speciation events, new body plans, biome colonisation, ecological challenges. Unlocking environmental tools.

**Long-term (weeks to months):** Each world is an "epoch." Culmination → fossil record → new epoch with better tools. Museum of past worlds grows. Meta-progression is growing mastery of shaping evolution.

**Collection layer:** Body plan archetypes (first flyer, first burrower, first symbiont), ecological milestones (first food web, first mass extinction, first recovery), evolutionary curiosities (convergent evolution across worlds).

## Simulation engine

### Architecture principles

1. **Pure maths, not real-time simulation.** Each tick = N generations. Population dynamics as equations with stochastic noise. No per-creature pathfinding. Renderer visualises state; doesn't drive it.
2. **Standalone testable module.** Pure TypeScript, no rendering dependency. Runs headless for time-jumps, offline progression, and unit tests.
3. **Environment is the story engine.** Climate shifts, seasonal pulses, catastrophes create narrative arcs. Creatures respond through evolution.

### Core systems

| System | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Population dynamics | Lotka-Volterra with stochastic noise. Birth/death rates from food, habitat, predation, competition. |
| Genome & mutation | Array of floats encoding traits. Reproduction copies with perturbation. |
| Speciation | Genetic distance clustering. Population splits when threshold exceeded. Geographic isolation accelerates. |
| Ecological interactions | Predation, competition, herbivory (v0.1). Symbiosis (v0.3+). |
| Environmental dynamics | Biomes from temperature + moisture + elevation. Seasonal cycles. Climate drift. Player-triggered events. |

### The pacing problem

Evolution sims tend toward collapse or stasis. Both fatal for idle games. Solution: an "edge of chaos" regulator that monitors simulation interestingness and nudges parameters. Not scripted events — parameter tuning. If speciation rate drops, mutation rate increases. If population drops dangerously, food pulse occurs.

### Event log & causal attribution

Every significant event recorded with causality: "Species X branched from Y because temperature gradient between coast and inland exceeded threshold. Key trait divergence: cold tolerance +0.3, leg length +0.2." This makes emergence legible.

## Social metagame

### Five layers (build sequentially)

| Layer | Feature | Infrastructure | Ship target |
|-------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | Species share codes | None — JSON in URL | v0.2 |
| 2 | Public species gallery | Simple API + DB | v0.3 |
| 3 | Robustness leaderboard | Tracking on gallery | v0.3 |
| 4 | Invasion events | Gallery + randomisation | v0.4 |
| 5 | Competitive ecology | World template system | v0.5+ |

### Species as portable artifacts

A shared species is a population-level definition: genome template + trait distribution + variation range. Self-contained, no dependency on source world state. Receiving world instantiates a founding population with realistic genetic diversity.

### Leaderboard categories

| Category | Measures |
|----------|----------|
| Most robust species | Highest survival rate across all worlds introduced to |
| Most disruptive invasive | Highest extinction-cause rate in receiving worlds |
| Most ancient lineage | Oldest species still alive across any world |
| Most convergent trait | Trait independently evolved in the most separate worlds |
| Most dramatic cascade | Single introduction causing largest downstream extinction |
| Most successful world | Highest sustained species diversity over time |

### Invasion balancing

Success depends on niche occupancy in receiving world. Full niches → invaders struggle. Empty niches (post-extinction, young world) → invaders thrive. Player controls: opt-in default, toggleable "open borders," configurable biosecurity.

## Target market

### Primary segments (launch order)

| Priority | Segment | Size indicator | How to reach |
|----------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| 1st | Idle/incremental community | r/incremental_games: 155K; 20–50M global MAU | r/incremental_games, itch.io, build-in-public devlogs |
| 2nd | Science & spec evo enthusiasts | r/SpeculativeEvolution: 155K; YouTube: millions | r/SpeculativeEvolution, YouTube devlogs |
| 3rd | Lapsed base-builder players | Factorio/Rimworld/ONI: 15M+ combined sales | Frame as "ecosystem Factorio in 10 min/day" |

### Positioning

For science-curious gamers who want the satisfaction of building complex systems without the time commitment of active play, Radiate is an idle ecosystem builder that lets you shape a world's environment and watch evolution unfold. Unlike Cell to Singularity (scripted progression) or The Bibites (constant active attention), Radiate combines genuine emergent simulation with idle mechanics, producing unique evolutionary stories that play out while you're away.
